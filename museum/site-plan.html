Overview

Purpose

The Gallery website will share important information for people who wants to visit museums in the Philippines. All things you need to consider before visiting the museum will be found in this website. It includes location, time schedule, and basic description of the museums. Also, one of the goals of this website is to promote the arts industry here in the Philippines

Audience

The target visitors of this website are for people who are considering to visit museums. The visitors may be, but not limited to, adults who are interested in learning history and arts. This website focuses on both desktop and mobile users.

Content

Home page

Welcome to The Gallery PH. Let us help you with everything you want to know about your trip. Find the exact location of the museum you are looking for. Know the schedule of the museum which includes timeslot and availability. Book a trip to the museum of your choice. The Gallery is an informative website that is open to all who wanted to know more about the cultural history of the Philippines. The website provides answers to your questions about the basic things to know about the different museums located in the Philippines. The National Museum of Anthropology stages the Philippine ethnographic and terrestrial and underwater archaeological collections narrating the story of the Philippines from the past, as presented through artifacts as evidences of its pre-history. The National Museum of Fine Arts is home to 29 galleries and hallway exhibitions comprising of 19th century Filipino masters, National Artists, leading modern painters, sculptors, and printmakers. Also on view are art loans from other government institutions, organizations, and individuals. The National Museum of Natural History houses 12 permanent galleries that exhibit the rich biological and geological diversity of the Philippines. It includes creatively curated displays of botanical, zoological, and geological specimens that represent our unique natural history. Situated at the center of the museum is a “Tree of Life” structure that proudly connects all the unique ecosystems in the Philippines, from our magnificent mountain ridges to the outstanding marine reefs. Pintô Art Museum is an exhibition space and contemporary museum located in the Philippines’ historic pilgrimage city of Antipolo outside of Manila. The museum was founded in 2010 to publicly exhibit the art collection of Filipino neurologist and patron of the arts, Dr. Joven Cuanang. The Mind Museum is the non-profit project of Bonifacio Art Foundation, Inc. It is dedicated to providing an extraordinary educational experience that inspires the public understanding of science. FAQs

Do the museums listed in this website allows walk-in visitors? Yes! All museums listed on this website accepts walk-in visitors. However, when visiting the museums in a large group (for around 20-30 persons), an advance booking may be required before visiting the museum. Is there a dress code that I need to consider? There is no dress code when visiting the museums listed. However, it is always best to dress approrioately to consider sensitivities of other visitors may have. Can I take pictures inside the museum? Yes, you are welcome to take a photograph of the permanent collection for your personal, nondistributional and noncommercial use. But take note that the use of flash is inappropriate and not permitted instide the premises. Food, bottled water, and ballpoint pens are not permitted inside the museum. There is a designated place for food and beverage near the museum. How long should I spend in the museum? Well, it depends on which museum you are planning to visit. You could spend 2 hours inside the National Museums and the Mind Museum. However, Pinto Art Museum is a massive area, and if you are planning to see the entire place, allot 4 to 6 hours with breaks in between. We entertain questions, suggestions and feedbacks. We definitely love to hear from you. If you want to us to help you with reservation to any of the museums listed on this website, feel free to send us an email, or you can call us.
